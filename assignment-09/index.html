How to Draw a Person in Charcoal

Materials | Steps

Materials

  • Sketch paper
  • Paper towel
  • Charcoal pencils
  • Compressed charcoal
  • Charcoal sticks
  • Moldable eraser
  • Workable fixative spray

Steps

Step 1: Choosing a reference

It is important to choose a good reference photo of the person you are drawing. The better
the quality of photo, the easier it will be to draw. Be sure to also choose a photo with a clear light source
and good contrast between the lights and darks. For my example I have chosen a photo of my Grandpa outdoors.

Step 2: Sketching

Begin your sketch by laying down a light gray background. Then use the charcoal sticks to lay down
a sketch of the person. Use large gestural movements and don't focus on the small details yet.
This is the time to focus on the proportions. Measure the width between different features on the face
to make sure everything is layed out accurately.

Step 3: Blocking in darks and lights

Once you have your sketch begin filling in the light and dark areas. Use the compressed charcoal to shade
the dark areas and use the moldable eraser to lighter the highlights. You can also use the paper towel to soften any
hard lines. Focus on the shapes of the shadows and highlights.

Step 4: Focusing on details

Once you have the darks and lights where they need to be you can focus on details. The pencil is useful for
drawing in small details like wrinkles, skin blemishes, hairs etc. The eraser can be molded into a fine point
to put in small lighter details in the hair, lips, and nose.

Step 5: Final touches

Finish up fine tuning the drawing. Once you are happy with it you can spray it with the workable fixative.
This will keep the charcoal from smudging but you can still make changes to it in the future if you need to.
